Self-Test Tasks: “Can You Still Do It?”

Module A3: When AI Helps vs. Hurts Your Learning

Instructions

  1. Choose the version that matches your course (or that your instructor assigns).
  2. Set a timer for 3 minutes per task (15 minutes total).
  3. Attempt each task without AI, notes, or textbooks. Use only a blank sheet of paper and a pen.
  4. After each task, rate your confidence on a 1-5 scale:
    • 1 = I had no idea where to start
    • 2 = I remembered the general idea but could not execute
    • 3 = I made a reasonable attempt but am not confident it is correct
    • 4 = I am fairly confident in my answer
    • 5 = I nailed it
  5. After all five tasks, complete the reflection at the bottom.

The point is self-awareness, not performance. A low score is useful information, not a failure.

Version A: Intermediate Microeconomics

# Task Confidence (1-5)
1 Set up a Lagrangian for utility maximization with a budget constraint. Write out the objective function, the constraint, and the first-order conditions.
2 Calculate consumer surplus from a linear demand curve. Assume: \(Q_d = 100 - 2P\), market price is $20. Find CS.
3 Explain the difference between a movement along the demand curve and a shift of the demand curve. Give one example of each.
4 Derive the profit-maximizing quantity for a monopolist. Assume: \(P(Q) = 50 - Q\), \(TC(Q) = 10Q\). Find \(Q^*\) and \(P^*\).
5 Interpret a price elasticity of demand of \(-0.5\). What does it mean? Is demand elastic or inelastic? What happens to total revenue if the firm raises its price?

Version B: Econometrics

# Task Confidence (1-5)
1 Write the population regression model for a difference-in-differences design. Define each term and identify which coefficient is the treatment effect.
2 Explain what omitted variable bias (OVB) is. State the two conditions required for a variable to cause OVB, and explain how to sign the direction of the bias.
3 Write a Stata command to run a regression of wage on education, experience, and female, with standard errors clustered at the state level.
4 Interpret a coefficient in a log-log model. If \(\ln(wage) = \beta_0 + \beta_1 \ln(education) + \varepsilon\) and \(\hat{\beta}_1 = 0.08\), what does this mean in words?
5 Explain the exclusion restriction for an instrumental variable. Why is it needed? Why is it not directly testable? Give an example of an instrument and explain the argument for why the exclusion restriction might hold.

Version C: Principles of Economics

# Task Confidence (1-5)
1 Draw a supply and demand diagram with a binding price floor. Label the surplus or shortage that results. Who benefits and who is harmed?
2 Calculate opportunity cost: A country can produce either 100 units of food or 50 units of clothing. What is the opportunity cost of 1 unit of clothing?
3 Explain why a price ceiling set below the equilibrium price causes a shortage. Use supply and demand reasoning.
4 Define GDP and list its four components (expenditure approach). Give one example of each component.
5 Explain what the Consumer Price Index (CPI) measures. How is it calculated? Name one limitation of CPI as a measure of inflation.

Reflection

How many tasks did you rate 4 or 5 (confident)? ______ / 5

How many tasks did you rate 1 or 2 (struggling)? ______ / 5

For any task you rated 1 or 2, is this a core skill for your course?

 

 

If you have been using AI for these types of tasks, has your unaided ability stayed the same, improved, or declined?

 

 

What does this tell you about where you might need to practice without AI?

 

 

Suggested follow-up: Repeat this exercise in 4-6 weeks. Compare your scores. If unaided performance is declining on core skills, revisit your AI use policy.
