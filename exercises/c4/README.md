# C4 Exercise Materials: Writing & Revision

These materials support the exercises in [Module C4: Writing & Revision](../../modules/c4-writing-revision.qmd).

## Contents

### Spot the AI Exercise

- **`spot_the_ai_exercise.md`** — Four paragraphs about immigration and wages: two written in a student voice, two generated by AI. Students identify which is which and explain their reasoning. Includes an answer key in collapsible tags.

### Writing Examples (Before / After / Targeted Edit)

These demonstrate the difference between a student's rough draft, an AI full rewrite, and an AI-assisted targeted edit. Each includes a comparison table highlighting what was gained and lost.

- **`writing_examples/results_paragraph.md`** — Reporting regression results from a job training evaluation
- **`writing_examples/lit_review_transition.md`** — Connecting two papers on neighborhood effects and economic mobility
- **`writing_examples/policy_implications.md`** — Drawing policy lessons from a CCT evaluation (where AI tends to overgeneralize)

## How to Use

### Spot the AI (15 min)

Use as an in-class warm-up or homework. Works well projected on screen for group discussion. The answer key is hidden in `<details>` tags so students can try before seeing the answer.

### Writing Examples (10-15 min each)

These pair with the module's exercise (Part 2), where students take their own writing through the same three-stage process:

1. Student reads the three versions and the comparison table
2. Student identifies what was lost in the AI rewrite and preserved in the targeted edit
3. Student applies the same approach to their own writing (from the module exercise)

The policy implications example is especially useful for research paper assignments, where students are tempted to let AI write the "so what" paragraph.

## Note

The student-written examples are constructed to sound like actual student writing — somewhat rough, opinionated, and specific. The AI examples are constructed to match typical AI output patterns (hedging, generic transitions, false balance, lack of specificity). They are stylized for pedagogical clarity; real AI output varies.
